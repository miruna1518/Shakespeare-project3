A midsummer night's dream

I - Everything begins in Athens, with Theseus and Hippolyta discussing the upcoming wedding. Then Egeus, Hermia, Lysander, and Demetrius enter the scene, with Egeus, Hermia's father, expressing his frustration that his daughter doesn't want to marry his chosen one, Demetrius. Theseus threatens Hermia that if she doesn't marry the man her father has chosen, she must either die or become a nun for the rest of her life. Hermia and Lysander, deeply in love, decide to leave Athens to live a happy life together. Meanwhile, the craftsmen plan to prepare a special comic play for the wedding of the Athenian rulers.
II - Act two takes place in a forest near Athens, where Oberon, jealous and possessive, wants to steal a child abducted by Titania. To achieve this, Oberon orders Puck to gather a magical flower to enchant Titania, so he can take the child. At the same time, Oberon notices Helena's suffering because Demetrius doesn't love her, so he commands Puck to use the same enchantment on Demetrius. Puck, however, confuses Demetrius with Lysander under the love spell. Consequently, Lysander, under the spell, falls in love with Helena, leaving Hermia alone, searching for her beloved who now loves another.
III - Meanwhile, the craftsmen begin rehearsals for the wedding play near Titania's bower in the same forest. Puck, hiding nearby, decides to give Bottom the head of a donkey, so that when Titania wakes, she will fall in love with the new creature. Oberon is pleased that Titania is enchanted but wants Puck to rectify his mistake of confusing the boys. He orders Puck to enchant Demetrius as well, so both men are now in love with Helena. Helena, not believing it, thinks it's a joke and a conspiracy between those three, leading to a quarrel between the women, similar to the fight between the men for Helena. Oberon, realizing his mistake, wants to undo all the love spells to bring everything back to normal. He sends Puck to fix it, and at the end of the act, Puck removes all love enchantments from the men.
IV - However, Titania is still in love with Bottom the donkey, and Oberon manages to steal the child he desired. Obtaining everything he wanted, Oberon orders Puck to remove this enchantment too. With the spell broken, Titania and Oberon reunite. Later, Theseus, Hippolyta and Egeus find Lysander, Hermia, Demetrius and Helena all asleep together in the forest. It was the day Hermia had to decide whether to marry Demetrius or face death. Coincidentally, Demetrius had fallen in love with Helena, and with the spell broken, Lysander loved Hermia again. Theseus, seeing this, allows the two loving couples to be happy and invites them all to the wedding that evening in Athens.
V - In the final act, Theseus and Hippolyta's wedding is celebrated, with the craftsmen performing their tragic comedy. Everyone is happy and reunited, as the young couples are finally allowed to love each other. The wedding festivities will last for 15 days, and the entire play is concluded by fairies blessing mortals.
